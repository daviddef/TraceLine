TraceLine is a single-player arcade puzzle game. No account, no login or registration, no
in-app purchase, no advertising, no user-generated content, no data collection. The app
requests no permissions of any kind. Everything is available from first launch — no
credentials or sample files are needed.

=== HOW TO PLAY — PLEASE READ FIRST ===

Press and hold ONE finger on the board and drag. A line follows your finger. Two rules:

1. DO NOT LIFT YOUR FINGER. The round ends the instant contact breaks.
2. The line may never cross itself.

This is the core mechanic, not a defect. If a round appears to end immediately, it is
because the finger left the glass — the game working as designed. The result screen states
the reason ("Finger lifted", "Line crossed itself", "Hit an obstacle", "Time's up").

To clear a level, cover the target percentage of the board (marked on the bottom bar)
before the timer expires.

To reach gameplay: tap "Play", then level 1, "Warm Up", at the top of the map. It has no
obstacles: cover 40% of the board in 60 seconds without lifting or crossing. Later levels
add hazards that fall or move across the board; touching one ends the round. "Cutters"
(vehicles on a dashed lane) sever the line instead of ending the round — the section they
will take is highlighted first. Shaded circles are shelters: hazards bounce off them.

=== INFORMATION REQUESTED ===

2. DEVICES AND OPERATING SYSTEMS TESTED
Physical device: iPhone 17 Pro (iPhone18,1) on iOS 26.0, installed via TestFlight. Also
exercised on the iOS Simulator: iPhone 17 and iPhone 17 Pro Max. The app is iPhone only,
portrait only, and supports iOS 16.0 and later.

3. PURPOSE AND TARGET AUDIENCE
A game of nerve and spatial planning for a general audience aged 4 and up. It is built on
one idea: your finger is the game. Rather than tapping buttons or steering a character,
the player draws the game state directly, and the drawn line becomes the obstacle they
must then avoid. The longer the line, the less room remains, so every stroke trades
progress against freedom. It offers a short, self-contained skill challenge — a round
lasts under a minute — with no account, no social pressure and no spending, entirely
offline.

4. SETTING UP AND ACCESSING THE MAIN FEATURES
No setup, credentials or sample files required. From the home screen:
- "Play" opens the world map. Level 1 is unlocked; each further level unlocks when the
  previous is cleared. 20 levels across 2 worlds.
- "Themes" offers four visual themes. Neon is available immediately, the rest unlock after
  World 1. Cosmetic only; it does not gate gameplay.
- "Leaderboard" opens Game Center. Optional — declining to sign in does not affect play.
No level need be completed to see the core loop: starting to draw demonstrates it at once.

5. EXTERNAL SERVICES, TOOLS OR PLATFORMS
Apple Game Center only, for an optional leaderboard and three achievements. No third-party
SDKs, analytics, authentication services, payment processors, AI services, advertising
networks or backend of any kind. The app makes no network requests other than Game
Center's own. Progress is stored locally in UserDefaults and removed with the app.

6. REGIONAL DIFFERENCES
None. The app behaves identically in all regions. It is not localised (English only), has
no region-gated content or features, and no server-side configuration that could vary by
territory.

7. REGULATED INDUSTRY OR PROTECTED THIRD-PARTY MATERIAL
Neither applies. TraceLine is not associated with any regulated industry and contains no
third-party material: every visual element — app icon, game art, levels and interface — is
drawn procedurally in code by the developer. There are no licensed images, audio,
trademarks or other protected assets, and no fonts beyond the system typefaces.
